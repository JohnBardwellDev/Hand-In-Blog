Rewiring the Brain

Today I am looking at two concepts that completely change how I approach learning to code: neuroplasticity and growth mindset.

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is just a scientific way of saying your brain is constantly rewiring itself. Every time you practice a new skill, your brain builds new physical connections. The more you practice, the stronger those pathways get until the task becomes automatic.

I like to compare it to my first few shifts working the bar at San Fran on a Friday night. At first you are completely overwhelmed. You cannot remember where the right glasses are, the till system feels like a maze, and the noise is completely distracting. But after a few weeks your brain wires itself for that exact environment. You pour drinks without thinking and navigate the till on autopilot. That is neuroplasticity in action.

Growth Mindset vs Fixed Mindset

Growth mindset goes hand in hand with neuroplasticity. It is the belief that your skills are not locked in at birth. A fixed mindset says you are either naturally smart or you are not. A growth mindset says you can build your intelligence through effort and repetition.

In coding you are going to break things constantly. If you have a fixed mindset, an error message feels like proof that you are not cut out for tech. With a growth mindset, an error is just feedback telling you exactly what to fix next.

My Learning Plan

Understanding this makes me realize that brute force studying is not always the best approach. I plan to focus on consistent daily practice and actually stepping away from the screen when I am stuck. When I hit a wall, that confusion is just my brain trying to build new pathways. I will step away, get some sleep, and let the rewiring happen.
