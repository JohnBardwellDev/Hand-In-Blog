Identity and Values

Sprint 1, 2026

If I had to sum up what I actually care about it would come down to three things: creativity, and the people around me. Family and friends are the reason I do most things. They keep me grounded and honestly they are a big part of why I decided to take on something like Dev Academy in the first place.

Creativity is something I have always valued even if I did not always have a clear outlet for it. I am drawn to things that let me make something from nothing, whether that is music, visuals, or now code. I think that is part of why web development clicked for me more than I expected. There is a creative side to it that I did not anticipate.

Something I have noticed about myself is that I do not learn well from just reading or watching. I need to be doing something. I need to be able to break it, fix it, and make it my own before it actually sticks. That is just how my brain works and I have stopped fighting it.

My values shape how I want to work too. I want to build things that look good and feel considered, not just functional. I care about the experience of using something, not just whether it works technically. That feels like something worth holding onto as I get further into this.
