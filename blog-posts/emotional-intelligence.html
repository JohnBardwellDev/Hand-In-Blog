Emotional Intelligence

April 13, 2026

Emotional intelligence is two things at once. It is observational, the ability to pick up on how someone is feeling even when they are not saying it outright. But it is also action based. It shows up in the small decisions we make in social situations, like making sure someone feels included in a conversation, or checking whether it is okay to bring a plus one to something intimate before just assuming. Those moments require awareness and consideration and that is EQ in practice.

The difference between EQ and IQ is something people get wrong a lot. Being good at processing information, retaining knowledge or solving logical problems has very little to do with being able to read a room or support someone who is struggling. They are genuinely separate things. You can be exceptionally intelligent in a cognitive sense and still be completely unaware of how your behaviour affects the people around you. IQ measures one kind of capability. EQ measures a completely different one.

EQ matters to me because I have seen what happens when it is missing. In team environments especially, a lack of emotional intelligence damages morale in ways that are hard to recover from. People stop communicating honestly, they disengage, and the whole culture suffers. It is not just a nice to have in a workplace, it is foundational to how well people work together.

On a more personal level I think about it like this. Holding space for someone when they are upset is just as important as feeding them when they are hungry. Both are basic forms of care. Recognising that and actually acting on it is what emotional intelligence looks like in real life.
