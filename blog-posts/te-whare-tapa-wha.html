Te Whare Tapa Wha and My Wellbeing

Bootcamp is intense. To avoid burning out I am using the Te Whare Tapa Wha model to build a wellbeing plan. It is a Maori health model developed by Sir Mason Durie that compares health to the four walls of a meeting house. If one wall fails the whole building is compromised. Here is how I am keeping my walls strong.

Taha Tinana: Physical Health

For me this starts with fixing my sleep schedule. Coming from years of working late night bar shifts my sleep was completely scattered. Now I need to be sharp during the day to learn code. My plan is to stick to a strict sleep routine and make sure I get outside to walk around Wellington every day instead of sitting at a desk for ten hours straight.

Taha Hinengaro: Mental Health

Learning JavaScript is incredibly frustrating at times. My main mental health strategy is to step away from the keyboard when I get stuck instead of brute forcing it. Taking a breather to clear my head is way better than staring at an error message until I get angry.

Taha Whanau: Social Health

It is really easy to lock myself in a room to study and completely drop off the map. To keep this wall strong I need to make active time to see my friends. Even just grabbing a coffee or hanging out to watch a movie helps me reset and remember there is a world outside of the code editor.

Taha Wairua: Spiritual Health

This does not have to be religious. For me it is about staying connected to why I am doing this bootcamp in the first place. I want to build cool things and eventually transition into the software and gaming industries. Taking time to work on my own creative projects or look at game dev stuff keeps me inspired and grounded in the big picture.
