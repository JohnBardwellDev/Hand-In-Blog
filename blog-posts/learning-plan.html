My Learning Plan

Sprint 2, 2026

The reason I am doing Dev Academy is pretty straightforward. I want to develop a skill that lets me work in a creative industry. I have always been drawn to things that combine technical ability with creativity and web development feels like one of the clearest paths into that kind of work.

My long term goal is to be working in a role where I am actually making things that look and feel good. Not just pushing buttons or maintaining someone else's code but genuinely contributing to the creative side of a product or project.

In terms of how I learn, I know pretty clearly that I need to be doing something to absorb it. Reading about a concept helps a little but it does not really stick until I have tried to use it, broken something, figured out why, and then made it work again. Having a real project to work on is what gets me going. Abstract exercises are harder for me to stay engaged with.

One thing I want to work on is managing my time better. I tend to go deep on things that interest me and lose track of the bigger picture, which has already caused me to fall behind. I am trying to get better at recognising when something is good enough and moving on rather than perfecting one thing at the expense of everything else.

I want to come out of this with enough confidence to actually build things on my own and enough knowledge to keep learning once the course is done.
