Problem Solving & The Mental Slump

Sprint 4 Reflection

Getting Stuck (Literally and Mentally)

I spent far too long staring at "Syntax Errors" during the FlipFlop kata. I’d be cruising along, hit a missing curly bracket { or a forgotten return statement, and instead of just fixing it, I’d let it stall me out.

But the bigger "stuck" wasn't the code; it was the isolation. I hit a point over the last few weeks where I just stopped reaching out. I was convinced I should be able to grind through it alone, but all that did was turn a one-hour problem into a three-day hurdle.

The "Eureka" Moment

My breakthrough didn't come from a specific line of code, but from finally breaking that silence. Realizing that the "15 minute rule" applies to my mental state, not just my terminal. If I’m spiraling for more than 15 minutes, the solution isn't more staring, it’s changing the environment or admitting to someone that I'm stuck.

My Problem-Solving Toolkit (Version 2.0)

I’ve had to refine how I handle things when the brain fog rolls in:

  • Writing it out in plain English When I can't think in JavaScript, I stop trying. I just write out the steps in normal words. It’s the only way to find the path forward when the logic feels like a mess.
  • Reading the Red: I’ve forced myself to actually read the error messages. They aren't insults; they’re directions.
  • Rubber Ducking (The Silent Version): Even if I wasn't talking to people, I started talking to the code. Explaining it out loud makes the stupid mistakes (like that missing bracket) jump out at you.

On Asking for Help

I have to be real: I didn't reach out to anyone for weeks. I fell into the trap of thinking that asking for help was a sign that I wasn't cut out for the bootcamp. Looking back at this sprint, I realize that the slump was actually the hardest "bug" to debug.

Moving forward into the in-person classes on May 4th, my goal isn't just to be better at JavaScript, it's to be better at not disappearing when things get heavy. The code is easier to fix when you aren't trying to do it in a vacuum.
